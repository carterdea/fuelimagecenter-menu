Introduction

Introduction
3.01.01

This section provides the core fundamentals for a contemporary and consumer-friendly forecourt. The aim is to show a simple and elegant solution for the majority of configurations of Mobil retail stations. There may be exceptions to these guidelines due to the various configurations in each market. Refer to the Exception process for any non-standard layouts to ensure any custom solutions adhere to the design intent of the new forecourt image.

Before any installation of each site you will need to prepare the station in the following manner.

  • All graphics, signs and secondary decals need to be removed from all columns and dispensers.
  • The site should be deep cleaned.
  • All broken equipment should be repaired or replaced.
  • The site should be freshly painted.
  • All work needs to be done safely. All provisions should be considered for working at heights, as required, and performed in a methodical and safe manner.
  • All electrical modifications should be permitted by the relevant jurisdiction.
  • Review designs against the minimum and recommended standards detailed in this document.

More details are available in the Station clean-up and preparation section that follows.

Minimum requirements
Sites are expected to install Recommended configurations where possible to maximize the impact of the new forecourt image and advertising effectiveness. However, if a site does not meet minimum volume and dispenser criteria as agreed between the country’s Retail Sales and Marketing organizations, then the site may install the “Lite” image configuration as agreed and defined by the local Retail Sales and Marketing organizations.

Key components
The forecourt is designed to enhance the consumers’ station experience. Key components are installed to grab the customer’s attention, welcome them to the site, enhance wayfinding, and display advertisements in a neat and organized way. These components include: Waves, Blades, Koalas, Number Wedges, Column End Caps, and Pump Toppers.

Components

Components overview
3.03.01

The forecourt is designed to enhance the consumers’ station experience. Key components are installed to grab the customer’s attention, welcome them to the site, enhance wayfinding, and display advertisements in a neat and organized way. These components include: Waves, Blades, Koalas, Number Wedges, Column End Caps, and Pump Toppers and are shown below.

  • Wave: A large inverted L-shaped aperture which rises up and over the dispenser that displays the Synergy Fuels image. Waves are always located on the first series of dispensers from the entrance to the site as a welcoming device. The Wave includes a Masterbrand logo near its base.
  • Blade: A horizontal element which displays the Synergy Fuels image. This is always located on the second tier of dispensers, unless the station has an awkward orientation, or fitting of the Wave would be compromised by other architectural factors. The Blade includes a Masterbrand logo at the end of the Blade, unless the Blade is positioned between columns.
  • Koala: Koalas are third tier signage apertures attached to canopy columns that display advertisements and key messages to consumers while pumping gas or returning to their vehicles from the backcourt.
  • Number Wedges: The number wedge is mounted on every canopy column above a dispenser, and clearly communicate the number of each dispenser, to aid customers and the cashiers when dispensing and paying for fuel.
  • Column End Caps: Column End Caps are installed only on columns wider than 500mm. These are placed behind the columns which hold the Blades when the column faces the backcourt or egress side of traffic flow (when the backcourt is adjacent to the dispenser configuration). They are never used on columns with a Wave or Breaking Wave unless the Waves or Breaking Waves are in a single starting gate formation. The Column End Caps include a Brand Logo.
  • Pump topper: On dispensers that have Blades, a Pump topper can be used in conjunction with a Koala to provide additional OSA apertures.

Components overview

Guidance for other elements:

  • On-site advertising (OSA) is streamlined to standard size and placement on Waves and Koalas on the forecourt.
  • Temporary beach flag signs and such are permissible for short-term launches
  • Sites with a car wash are permitted to post a car wash menu board, or card, on top of the dispenser. The purpose of the car wash menu board is to display the available car wash offerings that customers can choose from, along with prices. The car wash menu board should be no larger than 178mm x 229mm and should not be promotional.
  • Permanent perimeter signs affixed to structural items such as fences, walls, and foundations are permissable. Banners (fabric, tarp, vinyl) do not qualify as permanent signs.
  • Exceptions to these guidelines require approval by the Global Retail Fuels Marketing Director, after endorsement from the Advisory Committee (Retail Marketing Image Program Manager, Global Identity and Image Standards Advisor, Global Fuels Brand Manager).

Koala
3.03.04

Koalas are third tier signage apertures attached to canopy columns that display advertisements and key messages to consumers while pumping gas or returning to their vehicles from the backcourt. Koalas are installed on most, if not all, columns and are preferably fitted with their backs facing the backcourt.

3.03.04 page downloadEntire Forecourt section downloadEntire retail site guidelines download

The Koala is designed to adapt to a variety of column types. Below shows the approach with regards to whether the Koala has connectors or how they are positioned on the column itself.

On columns which are below 370mm, the connectors are fitted and the poster-frame is embedded in the Koala connector. Where the columns are wider than 370mm, the connector is not used and the poster frame is positioned in center between the Koala ears. On large columns, over 650mm, the Koala ears are centered on the sides of the column and a poster is positioned on the back. A Koala Connector or poster frame is not required if the configuration is such that the connector or poster frame faces a dispenser and the dispenser is less than 500mm away from the canopy column.

On columns which are 760mm wide and 250mm deep the Koala ears are mounted to the front edge of the column, with the back hanging out as shown. The frame is then centrally positioned between the Koala ears.

On all columns which are 250mm wide but over 650mm deep, the Koala ears are positioned with the connector to overhang the back of the column.

For narrow columns less than 250mm deep where the koala ears extend significantly beyond the edge of the column and the koala connector cannot be installed, the sight line to the central OSA frame may be obscured. In this case, the central poster frame may be relocated to the other side of the column.

For full-serve sites, the Koala ears on the secondary row of dispensers may be reversed to be angled toward the primary row of dispensers when the column is approached before the dispenser in the direction of traffic flow. The Koala connector or poster frame will then face the primary row of dispensers. As an option, a second central poster frame may be added to face the primary dispenser.

The Koala should be mounted at 1200mm from the forecourt mat for self-served sites (1100mm for full-served sites). The level of the Koala is always taken from the middle of the canopy column it is being fixed to, from the forecourt mat. The Koala should beangled toward the dispenser. When there is more than one dispenser around a single column, the Koala should be angled toward the dispenser with less OSA space (i.e. Blade on a Wave / Blade combination). In a Blade / Blade combination, the Koala should be installed so that the connector or back sign frame faces the backcourt.

The Koalas have been designed to be extremely flexible apertures. They have been developed to fit all column configurations listed below. On standard steel columns the Koala has a back which we call the Connector. This provides a 180-degree wrap of the column.

On columns with a width bigger than 370mm, when facing the backcourt, the Koala and Connectors are split so that a Koala is mounted on each side of the column, and a separate poster unit is placed between these two sides, providing the same OSA messaging to customers exiting the backcourt.

On round columns, a unique bracket will need to be fabricated in the field to accommodate mounting of the Koala.

For columns exceeding 370mm in width, the Koala connector is not required, and the poster frame is affixed directly to the column. The connector or poster frame is not required if the column is less than 500mm from the dispenser.

Component configurations
3.03.08

This section provides a guide to the installation of the key components as they relate to various forecourt designs and dispenser configurations.

3.03.08 page downloadEntire Forecourt section downloadEntire retail site guidelines download

The diagram below demonstrates the core measurements in positioning all of the apertures. These will vary from site to site but are based on some key principles which should always be observed.

The Wave must always have a gap of at least 290mm rom the top of the dispenser to the bottom of the Wave. To achieve vertical clearance a Wave extension module can be added in 200mm increments to clear the top of the dispenser. If the Wave is fitted over low-rise dispensers then this extension piece can be disregarded.

The distance of the Wave from the side of the dispenser should ideally be no less than 300mm. Where site configurations do not allow the minimum 300mm clearance, the Wave may be installed 100mm from the side of the dispenser. However, consideration should be made for access to the dispenser riser panel before installing less than 300mm.

Once the height of the Wave is established on the primary dispenser the Blades on subsequent secondary dispensers must be mounted at a height that maintains a horizontal line with the primary Wave.

The clearance of each Blade above the dispenser will increase or decrease relative to the slope of the forecourt but must maintain the horizontal line established by the main Wave.

Please note the measurements shown in the rendering are for guidance only. Actual dimensions may differ slightly depending on site specific configurations. Final dimensions will be available once the site specific survey is completed, and the site specific configuration is determined.

The basic principles of the layout strategy is based around the relationship of the dispenser and the aperture. Below shows the typical fundamentals which will determine the approach used for each forecourt.

Typically, the Wave will fit within any space below 900mm between a bollard and the side of the dispenser. If the bollard sits within this distance then the Wave will have to be located outside the bollard, with a minimum gap of 300mm. Double arching should never be used on any forecourt configuration.

There may be some locations where bollards can be removed and replaced. This should be assessed during site inspections. The Blade will need less consideration in relation to the bollard but all speakers, brackets and equipment that may inhibit the location of the Blade should be relocated or removed, if possible and safe to do so.

The main objective is to create a clear viewing approach to the Wave. Other solutions should also be explored to optimise this opjective, such as the cutting of horse shoe bollards into pole bollards or complete replacement /removal to ensure visibilty and also the protection of the dispensers are optimized.

Starting gate configurations
3.04.02

This is a classic starting gate configuration. The leading dispensers take the Wave or Breaking Wave in the direction of the traffic flow as shown. Koalas are mounted on the back of the columns facing the backcourt. The Column End Cap goes on the back of the canopy column to act as a visual directional cue to the OSA material below. 3.04.06

All configurations which have dispensers in front of columns take Waves on the entrance direction to the station, and the back set of dispensers would take Blades (see example below). Decal production standards
3.08.01

Two production methods are available for the production of grade decals and overlays. The recommended standard for grade labels and overlays is “second surface” and the minimum standard is “first surface”. Both standards can be achieved using either digital printing, silkscreen and a combination of both to meet specific artwork requirements effects and finishes.

To promote longevity in the service station forecourt environment, all decals must be finished with a clear coat or over-laminate to provide ample UV protection and have adequate petrol compatibility. Using readily available, white, 2 mil cast film with a petroleum compatible adhesive for all decals is highly recommended to meet the graphics standards specified. Check with your manufacturer to assure that they provide an extended warranty to meet these criteria.

Color management
Color management is a critical component of the high quality output required by ExxonMobil. All printers have different profiles and gammas and below is a way to achieve constant approved color references from various printers, inks and materials.

Color management should be applied to all processes and as a consistent quality check against the supplied swatch kit.

CMYK profiles must be adapted in the artwork to match the swatches and your printer’s profiles. Each color should be corroborated via a color spectrograph and have a delta tolerance of no less than 1.5 from the swatch kit for every color.

This approach will ensure color consistency across all sites and suppliers.

Recommended standard:
Second surface grade labels and pump overlays
This second surface configuration allows the image to be viewed ‘through a lens’ to protect it on potential high strike areas on the pump. The gauge of the material does a good job of withstanding abuse and hiding imperfections on the pump surface. The material is opaque enough to hide any image under these overlays.

This combination of material and ink are engineered for pump applications where chemical resistance and durability are required.

  • Material – .015 clear polycarbonate gloss / gloss (GE™ 8010 or equivalent)
  • First surface is screen printed with a UV based hard clear coat to promote scratch and chemical resistance and UV protection.
  • Graphics are screen printed second surface (face down) with UV inks and backed up with two coats of screen printed UV ink to maintain color integrity and opacity.
  • The printed sheet is laminated with 3M 7966 adhesive which is white on one side and silver on the other to promote opacity.
  • Graphic panels are steel rule die cut to OEM specifications.

Intended life expectancy: 5+ years against color fade or delamination

Power and lighting

Power and lighting overview
3.12.01

LED light strips are an essential element of the Wave and Blade apertures. The low-voltage (12V) LEDs establish a modern aesthetic that conveys a premium quality for motor fuels and retail sites. The LEDs should be illuminated 24 hours a day. Where restricted, LEDs at a minimum should be illuminated when canopy downlights are illuminated.

LED light strips are a required element unless local regulations or permitting agencies do not allow the additional lighting. Evidence of permitting restrictions must be provided to your local Territory Manager. Additional exceptions may be granted based on pre-defined criteria set by the country Retail Sales and Fuels Program Managers.

For sites where LED exceptions have been granted, a chrome strip is available as a substitute.

ExxonMobil has tested various options for connecting power to the Waves and Blades. The preferred option is to power the Waves and Blades using buried or hidden conduit. However, this may not be possible at every site.

Retailers should consult local contractors on the best option to provide power for their specific sites. This section includes specifications for possible power solution options.

  • If spare conduits exist, then below ground wiring from the building could be used.
  • If non-dispenser related power is available on the island, in the canopy columns, or on top of the canopy, then this could also be a power source for the Blade / Wave LED.
  • In the absence of the two options above, tension cables with Power Pods from the canopy have been developed as the preferred option for getting power to the apertures. The Power Pod with Tension Cable solution is preferred as an intentionally designed, aesthetic solution instead of installing new conduit down the exterior side of canopy columns. However, if existing conduit already exists on the exterior surface of canopy columns, and new conduit for the Wave or Blade would blend in with the existing conduit, then the use of conduit running along the external surface of the canopy column is acceptable.

The tension cable option is based on current available technology. Technological advances, particularly around solar cells, infrared technology, or advanced batteries, may provide more options in the future.

Other considerations
All electrical equipment installed should meet local regulations.

When possible, use an independent electrical circuit to power the Waves and Blades. A dedicated circuit will allow lock-out/tag-out for maintenance and illumination control independent from other circuits (canopy downlights, canopy fascia, etc).

Ensure local regulatory requirements are met if LED lights need to be connected to an emergency shut-off switch.

Mount a residual-current circuit breaker (RCCB) (30mA) (or GFCI circuit) at the main switch board.

Drilling and tapping preferred (rather than self-drilling / tapping screws). Be aware that electrical wiring and other pressure piping may be inside the canopy columns, and all drilling and tapping should be completed with caution.
